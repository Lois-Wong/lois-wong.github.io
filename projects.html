Projects

Gaita: A RAG System for Personalized Computer Science Education

Gaita is a conversational RAG system that generates personalized Computer Science learning pathways from open-access courseware. Inspired by my own transition into tech, Gaita makes CS education accessible to learners from non-technical and non-traditional backgrounds.


AI Symposium

We often hear that AI development needs perspectives from fields like the humanities and social sciences, but there is little clarity to what this actually means in practice. This symposium brings together AI practitioners/developers and experts from different fields to discuss bottlenecks to meaningful interdisciplinary AI collaboration and how to begin operationalizing it. Symposium proceedings will be published in our institutional repository, and we are establishing a Community of Practice to move from discussion to implementation. Call for proposals and further details will be announced fall 2026.


AI & Digital Citizenship Program

AI literacy is becoming a gatekeeping competency. Without it, people are excluded from professional opportunity, and more importantly, from participating in decisions about systems that impact everyday life. Addressing bias and harm requires contributions from different academic fields and the communities affected by these technologies, and current AI education resources do not support these learners.

This program will be accessible to learners without traditional technical backgrounds. Unlike existing AI education resources, this prioritizes civic participation, critique, and responsible use over technical career placement. It is being developed as anon-campus program, a teach-in open to the community, and MOOC.


AI from an Information Perspective (MOOC, In Progress)

This introductory AI course, currently being developed in collaboration with Sharesly Rodriguez, AI Librarian at SJSU, reframes AI through an information systems lens. It explores how challenges such as bias, data privacy, and consent are fundamentally information problems, and how advances in information and data continue to shape AI development.
